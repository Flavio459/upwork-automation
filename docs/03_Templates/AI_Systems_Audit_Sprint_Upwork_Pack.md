## Quick Qualification Checklist

- Is the opportunity about a real workflow, not just a task?
- Does the client mention audit, strategy, architecture, or discovery?
- Are there integrations, APIs, or multi-step processes involved?
- Can AI plausibly cover 90%+ of the execution with human oversight?
- Can the ticket support a consultative starting price?
- Is the client likely to buy clarity, not just implementation?
- Does the client seem open to diagnosis before implementation?
- Is there enough scope to justify a strategic audit, not a commodity fix?

## Decision

- Accept if the answer to most questions is yes and the client values a diagnosis-first approach
- Watch if the opportunity is real but the scope is vague
- Reject if it is commodity execution, cheap, or disconnected from business value

## Tone Guide

- Lead with judgment, not hype
- Keep language direct and restrained
- Avoid sounding like a generalist developer pitch
- Present the work as strategic advisory with technical depth
- Use implementation language only after the audit context is established
